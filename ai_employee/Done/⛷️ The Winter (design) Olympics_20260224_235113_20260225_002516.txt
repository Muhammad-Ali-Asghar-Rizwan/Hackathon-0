The best (and worst) design at Milano Cortina

Milano Cortina 2026 has delivered an impactful mix of design triumphs and unexpected missteps. Team Haiti and Team Brazil’s Opening Ceremony uniforms set a high-fashion tone, while Italy’s theatrical set design, complete with oversized stovetop espresso makers and cascading “paint” installations, celebrated national identity with flair. Individual athletes also made bold statements through design, from Alysa Liu’s evolving halo hair to custom (and controversial) helmets worn by Federica Brignone and Vladyslav Heraskevych.

But not every detail was a perfect 10/10: reports of malfunctioning medals and questionable costume choices sparked criticism. These moments highlight how Olympic design shapes perception, pride, and performance, visible on the world stage.

The rise of new nostalgia: Why tactile design is winning in 2026

This year, brands are moving past sterile minimalism toward a more tactile, human-centered aesthetic. From heritage-inspired “Elemental Folk” packaging to material-driven “Tactile Craft” finishes and the bold energy of “Distorted Cut” layouts and intentional imperfection is becoming a strategic advantage. These trends signal the care, authenticity, and individuality of brands in a saturated digital marketplace. For small businesses especially, warmth, texture, and story aren’t decorative extras, they’re powerful tools for differentiation and deeper customer connection.

Mouthwash Studio rewires T1 Energy’s identity

Mouthwash Studio redesigned T1 Energy at a key shift from legacy power to renewable leadership. Drawing from the geometry of solar cells, the team built a modular identity system that balances the brutalist industrial cues with unexpected softness. Through the custom typography, restrained colour, and motion-led UX, Mouthwash created a cohesive brand that combines the technical and human sides of T1 Energy. The resulting flexible and future-facing visual system mirrors how solar modules work: with precise, interconnected, and built to generate impact.
