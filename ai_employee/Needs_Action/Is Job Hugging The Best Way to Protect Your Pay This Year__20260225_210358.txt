Everyone’s clinging to job security like it’s 2020 again. But what if staying put is actually riskier than leaving? With layoffs still haunting inboxes and AI stealing your workflows, hugging your job tighter won’t save you – and it might just strangle your career. 🤗 The Job Switch Edition By Andrew Allen and Carla Dewing ⚡ Top Performers Don't Job Hug... Is job hugging keeping you safe, or keeping you small? It’s called job hugging - the silent career killer that looks like loyalty but feels like fear. You stop exploring. You cling to comfort. And before you know it, you’re standing still. But here’s what top performers know… fear isn’t a red light. It’s a highlighter. It tells you exactly where your growth is hiding. When others retreat, they refocus. When uncertainty hits, they don’t freeze - they move differently. They double down on skills. They audit their value. They recalibrate. Because job security doesn’t come from hugging your role tighter. It comes from becoming so capable, so clear, and so adaptable that you are the safest bet in any market. So, if you’ve been hugging your job like a safety blanket - maybe it’s time to ask: What could happen if you let go… and built something better? ☑️ Build career security, not job security: Staying put only works if you’re actively increasing your market value. That means portable skills, visible wins, and leverage that travels with you - not loyalty that only pays off if nothing changes. ☑️ Create momentum before you need it: Top performers don’t wait for layoffs, burnout, or AI disruption to force a move. They keep learning, exploring internal and external opportunities, and staying in motion so change never feels like a shock. ☑️ Turn fear into focus: Fear is inevitable in today’s job market. The difference is what you do with it. High performers treat fear as a sign to reassess, recalibrate, and act - not a reason to freeze or overwork themselves into exhaustion.  ✅ Do This Now Identify one way your current job is capping your potential. Audit your KPIs - are they aligned to career growth or company survival?
